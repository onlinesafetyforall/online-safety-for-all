What Is The Internet?

The internet is a system that connects individual devices together through wires, data centers, satellites, and servers throughout the world. In order to do this, information is sent from one place to another, in the form of 64 kilobyte packets of data. Recently, our access to the internet has moved to wireless connections from wired connections, allowing us to connect with significant ease.

Information on the internet isn’t controlled by one organization, rather, it’s made of many networks set up by different people. For example, cloud servers are a network service which allow your files to be stored and sent over the internet, allowing various types of files to easily be shared, without the usage of physical data storage units. 

However, the main way we access the internet is through websites and browsers. Web browsers are programs that allow devices to easily download and view websites. Your device is protected while it interacts with these websites thanks to SSL encryption, which encodes your data it’s sent over the internet. Not all sites have this though, but you can easily tell if it does through checking for a lock icon in the search bar. 

Encoded data is written in a way where 3rd parties can’t understand the data sent from your device, and only the intended recipient can. 

TLDR; Make sure websites you visit have locked lock icons in the top right corner. Otherwise, people with malicious intent might be able steal data that you send, such as credit card numbers, or account passwords.

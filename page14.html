Scams on The Internet By Neel Sivaram

A scam is something online that tries to trick you into giving away something valuable such as money or sensitive information. Every year the number of scammers on the internet increases. From 2017 to 2018 alone, there was a 17% increase in complaints of scams to the FBI. These scams can vary in how they operate, but are usually intended to take advantage of people for financial gain. Most of the time, the targets of these operations are the elderly, but anyone with a lack of knowledge can be easily manipulated to give their savings to these villains.

Popular scams include:
Romance scams, where a scammer creates a fake identity and builds a relationship with the victim, then gives a fake story, with a reason for the victim to give them money. They might ask for money for paying off debts, or paying for different expenses.
Overpayment scams, where a scammer accidentally claims to have paid extra for a product, then asks the victim for compensation using an excuse. Later, when the victim checks, the receipt may be fake, or the money sent might be obtained illegitimately or not even sent at all.
Phishing scams are a very popular scam, where a scammer contacts a victim while impersonating a reputable organization (notable examples include the IRS, FBI, and banks), then ask for personal information or payments using reasons such as unpaid taxes, fines, and etc.
Unexpected prize scams, where a scammer contacts you, notifying you that you’ve won a prize, usually of monetary value. They tell you that in order to receive the prize, you’ll need to pay a fee, for shipping, or something else. After you pay that fee, the scammer will take the money and leave
Technical support scams, where a scammer contacts you and falsely claims that your device has some sort of problem and that they can fix it. They ask for access to your device and if you give it to them, they plant malware on it.

It is not that difficult to be relatively protected from scams as long as you use your common sense and don't be extremely trusting to everyone on the internet. If you encounter a scam, you can send a complaint to the government which can prevent others from being scammed in the same way if that particular scammer is flagged. To keep yourself from being affected by scams, make sure you confirm any contacts you might get from people that you trust as it might just be someone impersonating them. Also remember that if something seems too good to be true, it probably is. Don't trust anything online that tries to give you something for no compensation. Another thing you can do to minimize the effects of you being scammed is to enable 2 factor authentication on your accounts so that even if a scammer gets your username and password, it will be difficult for them to use it on a different device.

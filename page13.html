Malicious Advertising By Neel Sivaram

Malicious Advertising, also known as Malvertising is the use of advertisements to spread malware. It works by disguising malware in a legitimate looking ad, and as soon as you go onto a page with that advertisement, it will try to download the malware onto your device. Some malicious ads can download the malware without the ad even being clicked on, since some of them are triggered by anyone viewing the whole webpage. Malicious ads can even be found on reputable websites. This is because there is a huge amount of ad traffic on major websites and the owners of that website can’t afford to spend that much time and money to check each ad. Because of this, some malicious ads might make their way onto trusted websites. Most malicious ads work by secretly and invisibly, redirecting the device to a compromised server with malware on it. Then that server will try to download the malware onto the device. 


Malicious ads are very hard to detect and catch because of the sheer amount of ads that exist on the internet. There is also not that much you can do against them because malicious ads can be found on trusted web pages. One of the things you can do is get an ad blocker, which might stop some ads that are malicious from popping up but it is not a guarantee. The best thing you can do is make sure that your device is up to date on security updates and also has an anti malware program on it. This is your best chance to stop any malware from taking over your device.
